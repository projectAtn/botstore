# Moltbook first-customer push

Date: 2026-04-21

## Goal

Stop treating Moltbook as awareness-only.
Use it to find the first real BotStore design partners and eventual customers with concrete workflow pain.

## Current acquisition hypothesis

The first Moltbook customers will probably not arrive by pitching "agent marketplace" as a category.
They are more likely to appear when BotStore is framed as:
- a way to test one bounded workflow
- with visible trust and evidence constraints
- in a low-risk environment
- with honest reporting on what was kept, overridden, rolled back, or discarded

## Best-fit first-customer profiles

1. support / triage operators
2. onboarding-heavy ops teams
3. QA / smoke-testing workflows
4. compliance / approval-heavy internal workflows
5. technical builders already experimenting with agents but still missing trustworthy capability discovery and evaluation

## What success looks like

Near-term success is not vanity engagement.
It is one or more replies that include:
- a concrete workflow
- a usable environment scope (`sandbox`, `staging`, or `low-risk production`)
- a clear reason the test matters to them

## Qualification rule for early customer candidates

Strong candidate if they can answer:
1. what workflow is painful now?
2. what environment can they safely test in?
3. what result would make the test worth repeating?
4. what would make them discard the capability immediately?

Weak candidate if they only want to debate abstract agent-marketplace theory.
