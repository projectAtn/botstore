# BotStore v3 — 5 Comparison Posts (BotStore vs Alternatives)

Format: launch-ready social posts with clear, defensible comparisons.

---

## 1) BotStore vs Generic Prompt Libraries
Most prompt libraries stop at text.

BotStore focuses on deployable packs with workflow intent, ownership clarity, and operational use cases (e.g., `inbox-triage-ops`, `customer-support-copilot`).

If you need production workflows, not isolated prompts, BotStore is the better starting point.

---

## 2) BotStore vs Build-From-Scratch
Build-from-scratch gives max control but high setup overhead.

BotStore gives a faster path for teams that need proven starting points, then customization over time.

Use scratch builds for edge cases; use BotStore when speed-to-value matters.

---

## 3) BotStore vs Single-Model Chat Apps
Single-model chat apps are great for ad hoc tasks.

BotStore is built for recurring workflows with named pack patterns, practical handoff logic, and domain-oriented packs like `sales-crm-autopilot` and `meeting-intelligence-pack`.

Chat helps you think. Packs help teams execute.

---

## 4) BotStore vs Uncurated Bot Directories
Uncurated directories optimize for volume.

BotStore prioritizes quality-screened, high-demand operational packs across support, revenue, compliance, and research.

Fewer dead ends. Faster decisions.

---

## 5) BotStore vs Agency-Only Custom Engagements
Agency builds can be strong but are often slower and costlier to start.

BotStore offers immediate, reusable pack foundations teams can pilot internally before committing to full custom programs.

Pilot first with BotStore. Deep-customize when needed.
